History

The history of ProjectSpeech.

 

Dates: 

March 27, 2017.

The first seed of ProjectSpeech is planted

 

ProjectSpeech was a pet project of Joseph Seohyun Lee's in the spring of 2017. ProjectSpeech started when Joseph watched a video on those with disabilities, and how much it impacts their lives. Through this experience, Joseph launched ProjectSpeech to help those that are not as fortunate an opportunity in a field of studies that Joseph calls "the future of the world."

ProjectSpeech was furthured developed thanks to the actions of Mr. Rogers, a civil engineering and IED high school teacher at West Lafayette Jr/Sr. High School. Mr. Rogers promoted creativity, innovation, and progress. ProjectSpeech would not have been created if it was not for Mr. Rogers.

Through ProjectSpeech, it is our hope to expand the knowledge of STEM and Computer Science to anyone, anywhere.



The Full History

Beginning in March 2017, ProjectSpeech was first inspired by Joseph Lee and the members of FIRST robotics Team 461. The robotics team was designing a prostetic a non-profit organization. Through team meetings, Joseph learned many people with disabilities were unable to participate in STEM activities. With the help of Jack Birkheimer on Team 461, ProjectSpeech was officially launched. Pamphlets were created and passed around the Purdue University campus, but it came to Joseph's knowledge it did not recieve much attention. As a programmer with knowledge in Javascript, HTML, and CSS, Joseph started creating a website to spread the word of ProjectSpeech not just on the Purdue campus, but to the entire world. While the website was undergoing development, Joseph was forced to move to Charleston, South Carolina during the summer of 2017, which threatened the future of ProjectSpeech. Joseph decided to continue the project without Jack Birkheimer and revamped ProjectSpeech in the Charleston area. With the help of members of FRC Team 4533, Joseph was finally able to complete ProjectSpeech's website on December 9, 2017. Ever since then this website has been the base of which ProjectSpeech uses to spread the word of voice-based programming and to help those who were not as fortunate as Joseph was.
